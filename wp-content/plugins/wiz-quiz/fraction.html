Advanced Math Example: Integral with Limits

Problem: Evaluate the Integral

Consider the following integral with limits:

\( \int_{1}^{4} \frac{3x^2 - 5x + 2}{x} \, dx \)

Step 1: Simplify the integrand by dividing each term in the numerator by \( x \):

\( \int_{1}^{4} \left( 3x - 5 + \frac{2}{x} \right) dx \)

Step 2: Now we can separate the integral into three simpler integrals:

\( \int_{1}^{4} 3x \, dx - \int_{1}^{4} 5 \, dx + \int_{1}^{4} \frac{2}{x} \, dx \)

Step 3: Solve each of the integrals:

\( \int_{1}^{4} 3x \, dx = \left[ \frac{3x^2}{2} \right]_{1}^{4} = \frac{3(4)^2}{2} - \frac{3(1)^2}{2} = 24 - \frac{3}{2} = \frac{45}{2} \)

\( \int_{1}^{4} 5 \, dx = 5(x) \Big|_1^4 = 5(4) - 5(1) = 20 - 5 = 15 \)

\( \int_{1}^{4} \frac{2}{x} \, dx = 2 \ln|x| \Big|_1^4 = 2(\ln(4) - \ln(1)) = 2 \ln(4) \)

Step 4: Combine all the results:

\( \frac{45}{2} - 15 + 2 \ln(4) \)

Step 5: Final simplified result:

\( \frac{45}{2} - \frac{30}{2} + 2 \ln(4) = \frac{15}{2} + 2 \ln(4) \)

$$ \frac{45}{2} - \frac{30}{2} + 2 \ln(4) = \frac{15}{2} + 2 ln(4) $$
